          <p class="_subtitle">UIC's Academic Health Enterprise</p>
    
              <p>The <strong>University of Illinois Hospital &amp; Health Sciences System (UI Health)</strong> provides comprehensive care, education, and research to train health care leaders and foster healthy communities in Illinois and beyond.</p>
<p>A part of the <a href="http://uic.edu/">University of Illinois at Chicago (UIC)</a>, UI Health is a <a href="https://hospital.uillinois.edu/">clinical enterprise that includes a 465-bed tertiary care hospital, 21 outpatient clinics</a>, and 11 federally qualified <a href="https://hospital.uillinois.edu/patients-and-visitors/mile-square-federally-qualified-health-center">Mile Square Health Center</a> locations. With campuses in Chicago, Peoria, Quad Cities, Rockford, Springfield, and Urbana, the health system  includes the academic and research activities of the seven UIC health science colleges:  <a href="http://ahs.uic.edu/">Applied Health Sciences</a>, <a href="https://dentistry.uic.edu/">Dentistry</a>, <a href="https://medicine.uic.edu/">Medicine</a>, <a href="https://nursing.uic.edu/">Nursing</a>, <a href="https://pharmacy.uic.edu/">Pharmacy</a>; <a href="http://publichealth.uic.edu/">the School of Public Health</a>;  and <a href="https://socialwork.uic.edu/">the Jane Addams College of Social Work</a>.</p>
<p>UI Health is dedicated to the pursuit of health equity.</p>
